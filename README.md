# Excel Outlier Analysis Project

## Business Problem
The Chicago Cubs organization wanted to determine whether any beer vendors were pocketing cash instead of reporting full sales. 
The goal was to compare actual beer sales to projected sales overall and by employee, and determine whether there were any statistical outliers that might indicate theft or underperformance.

## Dataset Description
The dataset included the following variables:
- Shift number
- Week number
- Employee number
- Actual sales
- Projected sales
- Difference between actual and projected sales

## Analysis Process
To analyze the data, I performed the following steps:
1. Created Pivot Tables to compare average actual vs projected sales by shift
2. Created Pivot Tables to compare average actual vs projected sales by employee
3. Calculated the difference between actual and projected sales
4. Calculated Z-scores to identify statistical outliers
5. Identified employees whose sales were consistently below projections
6. Analyzed sales patterns by shift and week for the most problematic employee
7. Wrote a business recommendation email summarizing findings

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Z-Score Analysis
- Data Analysis
- Business Analysis

## Key Findings
- Most employees performed close to projected sales
- Employee 15 had consistently lower sales than projected
- Employee 15 was identified as a statistical outlier using Z-score analysis
- Employee 34 was slightly below projections and may require monitoring
- Employee 15's sales were below projections across most shifts and weeks

## Business Recommendation
Employee 15 should be monitored further to determine whether the issue is theft, poor performance, or shift-related differences. 
Additional monitoring and comparison across future shifts is recommended before taking disciplinary action.
